<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify">ALAN <span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> </span>RUSH is a leading British historical researcher, consultant and writer with a special interest in the <st1:place st="on">Middle East</st1:place>. Based in London and Cairo he is currently working on Egyptian social history and the history of the <i style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal">ahl al-bayt </i>(family of the Prophet Muhammad) focussing on the Sharifs of Mecca and the affairs of the Hashemite royal families of Jordan and Iraq.</p>  <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><span lang="EN-GB"><o:p> </o:p></span></p>  <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><span lang="EN-GB">Born of Anglo-Irish and Danish parentage in July 1936, Alan Rush first worked as a reporter on the <i style="mso-bidi-font-style:normal">Daily Express</i> newspaper (Beaverbrook Press) in <st1:city st="on"><st1:place st="on">London</st1:place></st1:city>. Later, he joined the editorial staff of <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">The Authors’ and Writers’ Who’s Who</b>, <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> </span>Burke’s Peerage </b>and <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal">The International Statesman’s Yearbook </b>(Mercury Publications)<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">. </b>In the <st1:country-region st="on"><st1:place st="on">United States</st1:place></st1:country-region> he taught English literature before becoming a style-editor with the <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal">Encyclopedia Americana</b>.</span></p>  <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><span lang="EN-GB"><o:p> </o:p></span></p>  <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><span lang="EN-GB">He first visited the <st1:place st="on">Middle East</st1:place> in 1970. Office, British Library (London), the National Archives of India (Delhi), the Middle East Centre, Saint Antony’s College (Oxford), the National Archives (Washington), the Ministre des Affaires Etrangeres (Paris and Nantes) and the League of Nations (Geneva). </span></p>  <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><span lang="EN-GB"><o:p> </o:p></span></p>  <p class="MsoNormal"
